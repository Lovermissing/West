YeXi Lake was built in the 1980s.

At that time, the central area of the west campus of USTC was still a messy low-lying area.

Teachers and students participated together in dredging and renovation, turning it into a garden landscape.

There is an island in the lake, connected to the south bank by a wooden bridge called the Nine-Curve Bridge.

A small pavilion stands on the island, named Yixin Pavilion.

The northeast corner of the lake is Fanghua Garden.

Tall metasequoia trees grow in YeXi Lake, and plum and peach trees are planted along the lakeside.

Winter brings wild ducks to spend the season here.

What is particularly unique is its name—YeXi Lake.

Perhaps intentionally following the naming of West Lake,

this place may not have the fame of the paradise in Suzhou and Hangzhou,

but the scenery is similar—"Except for Hangzhou, there is also YeXi Lake."
